# AI Agents

## Introduction

AI Agents are software agents that can be used to perform tasks. They can be used to automate tasks, provide information, and interact with users. 

AI agents can be classified into different types based on their capabilities and functionalities.

They can enable decision-making, problem-solving, and learning. They can be used in various applications such as customer service, virtual assistants, and autonomous systems.
LLM Agents can reason and solve complex problems by breaking down them down into smaller, more manageable subproblems. 
They can also learn from their interactions and improve their performance over time.

They can also act on a problem by interacting with tools.

Tools are external pieces of programs that model can decide on the actions to take.

### Multi-Agent Systems

Multi-agent systems are systems that involve multiple agents working together to achieve a common goal.
Multi-agent systems can be used to solve complex problems, such as traffic management, resource allocation, and collaborative tasks. They can also be used to simulate social interactions and behaviors.

### Types of AI Agents

1. **Reactive Agents:** These agents respond to stimuli in their environment without maintaining an internal state. They make decisions based on the current input and do not have memory of past interactions.

2. **Proactive Agents:** These agents have an internal state and can make decisions based on past interactions. They can learn from their experiences and improve their performance over time.

3. **Deliberative Agents:** These agents have a more complex internal state and can plan their actions based on their goals and the current state of the environment. They can reason about their actions and make informed decisions.
